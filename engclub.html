    CU China Dance

    Columbia Chinese Dance Academy (CU China Dance) was established in September 2013. The Club welcomes all the students interested in Chinese dance culture, the Chinese folk dance, classical dance, with its goal to promote Chinese dances on Columbia campus and the New York region. Since its inception, the CU China Dance performed at Columbia University Night Market, CU TASA philanthropy banquet, 2014 Columbia Spring Festival and other large events. Dancers demonstrate the unique Chinese culture through the form of performing arts show to the audience.

    Rhythmic - Columbia Chinese Dance Academy dancers hope to find beautiful things and get the true meaning of life through dancing. We look forward to every interested students to join.

    CU Crosstalk

    Columbia University Crosstalk was established in September 2012. Our members were consisted of participants from the previous CUCSSA Spring Festivals, including Columbia undergraduate and graduate students who are interested in creative performances. Now, there are more than ten members who performed nearly 10 plays. Over the past few years, we met with famous crosstalk artist, Guo Degang, who visited Columbia. We also performed with rookie star Wang Zi Jian who studied at New York. During finals period, we held a number of comic specials for the students to relieve stress. Even more at the annual Columbia Spring Festival Evening, we served as the grand finale. We were invited to perform at four universities in Philadelphia, Princeton University and USC.

    We welcome all students interested in the creation of comic performers to join us and share their skills to entertain Columbia students.

    Columbia Music Club

    Columbia music club is one of the musical student activity groups of CUCSSA. Our club serves the international students，scholars and Chinese in New York. We aim to bring the development of the music to a new level and provide more interesting musical activities to more people. We also dedicate to cultural communication with the society through music activities on and off campus. You are welcome to join us if you love music. You do not need to be skilled in particularly one musical field All you need is passion and a soul for music.

    ISPA

    ISPA( I-SPA) is the first student association that focus on the psychology of international students. It is where you can talk about academic issues, and it is also a place you can mingle and make friends. ISPA, stands for International Student Psychology Association was founded by Jiang Fan in 2012, a 2013 graduate from psychology major. The title of the association speaks for itself. We aim to provide a platform for students of psychology background to communicate with us and display their professional knowledge. We also welcome students who are interested in psychology to join us and learn more about psychology.

    CUCSSA Bastketball Club

    CUCSSA Bastketball Club is one of the most popular and well-known clubs. CUCSSA founded this club to promote the sport of basketball, enhance physical fitness and facilitate communication and exchange between students. The Basketball Club organizes at least one practice per week on every Wednesday evening or Friday afternoon, depending on the availability of Levien Gym. Club members also organize additional basketball practices.

    CUCSSA Fitness Club

    CUCSSA Fitness Club is founded by CUCSSA to promote workout activities, enhance physical fitness, develop healthy and active lifestyles, and facilitate communication and exchange between students. We welcome all students to share their fitness plans and insightes as well as participate in our training programs organized twice a week. Men’s training mostly focuses on strengthening. Women’s training is guided by volunteers and focuses on the use of fitness equipment and different aerobics moves. During the spring semester, programs are held every Thursday at 8:30pm and Sunday at 4:30pm and last about an hour.

CU Chinese Football Team

“Going out of the boring office and getting rid of busy school work, guys are dasing at the football stadium like wind.” ----Xiazhu Su, journalist of US China Express

“A flag among American students.” ---- iiWii (Weiming BBS)

Yes, that’s CU Chinese Football Team! We win as a tem! If you have a game with CU Chinese football team in any competition in east America, you should be very careful because you meet the most competitive team here. Honor is the reward of practice as well as the symbol of team wrok.

In 2013, CU Chinese Footbal Team won the championship of NYC One Day Tournament (8-people, other teams are all American teams), silver medal of East America Cup(11 people), and unprecedentedly won the championship of Ivy League Cup, the highest competition among Chinese people in east America by wining seven other universities. In 2015, CU won the championship of Ivy League Cup again and became the only team in history that had won the championship twice in a row.

CU Chinese Football Team has been reported by China Daily, Sinovision and China Express. These honors are owed to the effort of CU alumni as well as the support of CUCSSA. If you have an excellent sense of football, solid football skills and unyielding spirit, please join the CU Chinese Football Team. Let’s fight together!

No matter what you are good at, welcome to CU Chinese Football Team because football is our common interest.
