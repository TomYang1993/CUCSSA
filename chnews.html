    CUCSSA Official Resume Statement

    Columbia University Chinese Students and Scholars Association (CUCSSA) has officially re-registered as a student organization on April 21, 2015 after receiving confirmation from university officials. At this time, CUCSSA will be running under more strict internal supervision as well as receiving more support and guidance from school. From this day on, CUCSSA will resume its services including newsletter, weixin, weibo, and online forum. The New Students Guide Program for 2015 will also start immediately. This year’s program will contain several events such as online Q&A, printed version of new students guide, new students meeting in China, airport pickup, and new students welcome lunch. Every prospective student should pay attention to above events for a smooth start at Columbia.

    Moreover, in order to understand the true needs of Chinese students in Columbia University as well as to provide better services, CUCSSA and ISSO will release a survey to collect information from all the Chinese students. We hope everyone can take some time and finish this survey to help us improving our work.

    Last, we would like to appreciate everyone that helped us in perfecting and resuming our services. CUCSSA will continue to serve all Chinese students in Columbia University with proper standards and a more transparent environment. We shall work actively with school officials to focus on creating better academic and living environments and updating our services for our students in the future.

    哥大中文论坛全面升级改版

    大家好。经过网络团队的日夜建设，CUCSSA BBS于8月15日全新改版，同时老版BBS也会关闭。建设团队广泛征集了原有论坛用户的意见与建议，针对原有存在的各种问题进行改进与完善，同时，也对论坛进行了全新的布局和美化。希望大家会喜欢这个新家。

    以下为主要的新特性：

    全新布局，全新美化

    新版论坛重新布局，多余板块合并，节省空间，提高浏览效率。 顶部的logo加入“回到论坛主页”功能，方便浏览者快速导航。 新增搜索条，方便大家快速根据关键字搜索论坛资源。输入栏右方可以设置搜索帖子内容，或者搜索特定用户。方便快捷。 导航栏右侧新增“快捷导航”链接，一键浏览帖子，好友列表，收藏帖子等等。细节不再错过。 新增板块图标。板块主题一目了然，提升用户浏览体验。 新增板块“大杂烩”。这里就是传说中的水区。所有的水帖，心情帖，八卦新闻，这里就是新家。

    Columbia 邮箱注册要求

    广大网友对老论坛提出的最大问题就是外来的广告帖。因此，Webteam重点攻坚这一论坛顽疾，给大家创造一个良好的讨论空间。 我们要求所有的注册用户都要输入学校邮箱。系统会发出一个验证邮件。没有通过验证的用户，只可以浏览论坛帖子，但是无法发表任何回复与新帖。我们相信，广告党从此可以从论坛上销声匿迹。

    支持qq帐号登陆

    大家现在可以把自己的qq号和已通过验证的论坛帐号绑定。这样，每次登陆论坛就可以输入自己的qq帐号信息。方便省事。同时，也可以设置帖子和回复同步到腾讯微博和qq空间，更有助于把有帮助的信息传播给更多的人。 如果只用qq帐号登陆而暂未通过邮箱验证的用户，只可以浏览论坛，但是无法发帖与回复。

    支持手机浏览

    新版论坛支持简易版手机浏览。iPhone和安卓手机用户也可以下载手机App，享受更好的浏览体验。只要通过手机浏览论坛，就可以发现下载App的方法。

    支持宽屏／窄屏切换

    右上角的“切换到窄版”或“切换到宽版”提供两种近似的论坛显示方法，同时满足宽屏用户大屏浏览的需要，也保障普屏用户的顺畅浏览。

    更快访问速度，更安全可靠

    新服务器已经全面迁移至美国境内，采用云平台技术，访问速度也将更快。同时网络采用多种安全技术，确保安全可靠。

    CUCSSA Freshman Meet-and-Greet

    In the afternoon of July 4, 2015, Columbia University (CU) Beijing Freshman meet-and-greet was successfully held in the No.1 teaching building of Tsinghua University. The attendees are special guest, CU alumni Chonghan Fan, and other guests Weizhi Shao, Kai Liao, Xi Chen, Bing Wu and the Ph.D. student Chenru Lv and MA student Boyu Zhang.

    First, CUCSSA’s former vice president Lijing Cai introduced CUCSSA and New York comprehensively, including composition and structure of CUCSSA, the important events of CUCSSA, for example, Freshman caring program, Lecture Room, New Year gala, Outlook Forum, and etc; he also explicitly talked about the weather, transportation and other basic necessities of life in New York. This helped new students prepare for living in a global center and metropolitan. Meanwhile, he specifically emphasized what students need to prepare for enrollment and living overseas by giving a checklist.

    Subsequently, Hanchong Fan shared his insightful thoughts he had when he studied at CU according to his firsthand experiences. He told fellow students how to efficiently promote themselves and to be their own Bo Le. The three upperclassmen, Weizhi Shao, Xi Chen and Bing Wu communicated with freshmen their own perceptions when they were at CU and encouraged them to positively face challenges and seek opportunities from the perspective of both life and study. Kai Liao also came up with the idea of making social events reappearing to broaden self’s horizons.

    This CU Beijing Freshman greet-and-meet is rich in its contents with a warm and lively atmosphere. Freshmen actively raised valuable questions related to academy, work and CUCSSA life. The guest speakers respectively provide exhaustive answers and suggestions. After the meeting, freshmen and alumnus interacted with CUCSSA members and freshmen expressed their eagerness to participate in the upcoming events of CUCSSA.

    CUCSSA Successfully Held the New Student Meetup on Sept. 13

    On September 13, 2015 at 1PM, CUCSSA held the New Student Meetup at the International House, welcoming about 300 new incoming Chinese students. As one of the New Student Care Program, this New Student Meetup event aims to answer questions that new students have at the first week of school, explains to them the Columbia environment and life in New York City, and facilitates new students’ adaptation in America.

    At 1PM, the New Student Meetup event started on time. At the beginning, the Associate Provost David Austell from Columbia University International Office and Shuyi Wu, the president of CUCSSA each had their opening remarks, expressing their welcome to new students. Yiwen Zhang from the Bussiness and Career Department of CUCSSA explained the department’s responsibilities, which include negotiating sponsors and holding career events. Following with Minghao Cheng and Peipei Yang of Community Development and Engagement explained how CUCSSA collaborates with Columbia University, other academic institutions and organizations. Li Wang from Media and Public Relations Department explained CUCSSA’s media platform and its collaboration with media workers, including how to establish good image of CUCSSA. Peizhe Li, the vice president of Social and Cultural Affairs Department, explained CUCSSA’s branding events in detail, including the New Student Care Program, Columbia China Forum, CUCSSA New Year Festival, and CUCSSA’s cub-committees. He welcomed students’ participation in any of these events and activities. Students on site expressed benefited much from this event, and expressed their interests in participating in the future events.

    The New Student Meetup also had the honor to invite Mrs. Jie Ding, the Education consul of Chinese Consulate in New York. She read the letter from Yongji Xu, the Education Conselor of Consulate General New York, giving students sincere welcome and greetings. Then Ms. Ding explained Chinese Consulate in New York in detail. Afterwards, she demonstrated Chinese Consulate in New York’s different services to Chinese students studying abroad, including emergency contact and protection from the Chinese Consulate. Ms. Ding specially emphasized the awareness of self-protection and extra attention to traffics. Guests who were present also include Lawyer Lantao Sun from Ross, Legan, Rosenberg, and also Xiaohua Hu from New York Chinese Service Center for Scholarly Outreach Group, who explained services provided by Scholarly Outreach Group, and emphasized the green channel services offered for Chinese students studying abroad. Later on, consultant from EXL Service, INc., former Barclay analyst, and also notable alumni Guoke Yang shared his job seeking experiences in America. Talent Jungle also made a presentation on how to network and his life experiences.

    After each guest’s speech, CUCSSA also presented crosstalk show from CUCSSA’s Crosstalk Club. There were continuous laughs and applauses. After wondering shows, everyone comes to the Low Library to have Dinner buffet, make friends, and register for CUCSSA’s different clubs.

    The New Student Meetup created a enriched, lively, and created a warm atmosphere. After the meetup, new students and members of CUCSSA were well interacted, and expressing their benefits from this event and looking forward to joining future CUCSSA events.
